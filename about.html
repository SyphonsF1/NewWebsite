About Syphons F1 in Schools:

We are a professional F1 in Schools team based in Mackay. The team was founded in 2019 by Luke Eldridge and Joe Fowler. Both of them being good friends at the time made it the perfect team for them. That same year they went onto desinging a car and creating a folio for the 2019 Mackay Regional Finals. Success was found at the regional finals and Syphons Successfully placed 3 of 50 other Cadet Class teams. Joe and Luke went onto participating at a state level where they placed 6th in the state finals. However states being the furthest a team could go in the cadet level, that was as far as they would go for the year. The next year Syphons would go onto becoming a development team and recruiting 2 new members, Tyler Smith and Nicholas Doyle. Nicholas would become the newly appointed team manager while Tyler would take Luke's place becoming the Design Engineer and Luke would become the Manufacturing engineer, Joe would be graphic designer that year. They would then again find success and find themselves onto the State Level, however would not progress from there. It is now 2021 and Syphons are now a professional F1 in Schools Team. We have competed at a national level and will be going to State Finals very soon. We hope to seek success and work towards National or even potentially World Finals. What is F1 In Schools?

F1 in Schools is an international STEM (science, technology, engineering, mathematics) competition for school children (aged 11–19), in which groups of 3–6 students have to design and manufacture a miniature car out of the official F1 Model Block using CAD/CAM design tools.
